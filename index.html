Finance Terms Explained
APR — Annual Percentage Rate
The yearly cost of borrowing including interest and fees. Lower APR = cheaper deal. Used car PCP is typically 6–12% APR with good credit.
PCP — Personal Contract Purchase
Deposit + monthly payments for 2–4 years, then a "balloon" GMFV payment if you want to keep the car. Most popular UK car finance type. You don't own the car until the final payment.
HP — Hire Purchase
Higher monthly payments than PCP but you own the car outright once all payments are made. No balloon payment. You build equity from day one.
Section 75 Protection
Paying any part of a £100–£30,000 purchase by credit card means the card provider is jointly liable for issues with the seller. Vital protection for used car buyers.
Outstanding Finance Risk
If a car still has finance owed, the lender holds a legal interest. Buying a car with outstanding finance means you could lose the vehicle even after paying. Always run an HPI check.
Part Exchange Value
What a dealer will offer for your current car. Typically 10–20% below private sale. Use our estimate as a negotiating benchmark.
Voluntary Termination (VT)
Under the Consumer Credit Act, once you've paid 50% of the total amount payable on a PCP/HP deal, you can return the car without further payments — as long as it's in good condition.
