Finance Terms
APR
Yearly cost of borrowing. PCP typically 6–12% with good credit.
PCP — Personal Contract Purchase
Deposit + monthly payments + optional balloon to own. Most popular UK type.
HP — Hire Purchase
Higher monthly payments but you own outright once done. No balloon. Build equity from day one.
Section 75 Protection
Pay any part of £100–£30,000 by credit card and the card provider is jointly liable for issues.
Outstanding Finance Risk
If finance is owed the lender can repossess even after you've paid. Always run an HPI check.
What is Representative APR?
The APR shown by lenders must be offered to at least 51% of successful applicants. Your actual rate depends on your credit score, income and existing debts. Always get a personalised quote.
Early Repayment
Under Consumer Credit Act you can settle a finance agreement early. Lenders can charge up to 58 days interest as an early repayment charge. Worth calculating if rates have fallen.
Balloon Payment (PCP)
On PCP the balloon (Guaranteed Minimum Future Value) is set at the start. If the car is worth less at the end you hand it back. If more, you own equity. Never miss a payment — the lender owns the car.
GAP Insurance
If the car is written off your insurer pays current market value — not what you owe. GAP insurance covers the shortfall. Especially important in the first 2-3 years when depreciation is steepest.

UK Mod Legality Guide
Quick Reference
Generally Legal & Insurable
Alloy wheels, lowering springs, performance exhaust (if not decat), cold air intake, sports air filter, window tint (front ≥70%), tow bar, short shifter, bucket seats.
Declare to Insurer — Policy May Change
ECU remap, coilovers, big brake kit, LSD, intercooler, turbo upgrade, supercharger, anti-roll bar, sports clutch, carbon bonnet, body kit, wrap/respray.
Illegal on Public Roads
Decat pipe (fails MOT emissions), nitrous oxide on the road, roll cage without FIA certification, window tint under 70% (front). Always check DVSA regulations before fitting.
Always declare mods to your insurer — failing to do so can void your policy. Prices and regulations change — verify with a specialist before fitting.
